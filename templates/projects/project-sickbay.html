Sickbay Analytics: Top 100 Most Popular Health Services Costs Visualization Tool

Back
Sickbay Analytics is a D3.js vizualization tool that allows prospective US patients or caregivers to acquire relevant, filtered government data points of health care costs for the top most common medical services, by state or hospital.

PROJECT DETAILS
Need: Costs in healthcare are increasing at a rapid rate, and patients are expressing difficulties accessing the cost for a particular health service prior to their visit. Government provided data is readily available (data.gov), but is not accessible or visualized in a way that is useful to the end user.
Objective: Develop an easily accessible, web visualization tool that allows prospective US patients or caregivers to inquire average health care costs for the top most common medical services, by state or hospital.
Approach: Designed a D3.js visualization tool, allowing the users to filter to relevant and useful information. Through my design work I explored increasing data transparency, especially in areas of rising costs.
Project Duration: 3 months; Fall 2015
Group Members: Joanne Bruno, Bradley Abbott
My Role(s): UI/UX Designer, Front-End Developer, Technical Documentation
Final Result: Complete, interactive visualization tool: LIVE WEBSITE

UX Process

I. Plan, Identify, Understand

Health care costs are rising and one of the top challenges the healthcare industry is facing, is the lack of transparancy. There are plenty of data sets available on data.gov, however, these data assets are not visualized in a meaninful manner that provides data insights.

In order to tackle this problem, my team member Bradley and I set out to understand how the end user may benefit from a software solution that would enable them to better understand health care costs trends. We focused first on a prospective patient in the United States, seeking to become more informed on the cost of their health care service in their state.

Storyboarding

In order to best visualize the data, and make it interactive and customizable to the user's needs, I chose to design the tool's visualizations using Data Driven Documents, or better known as D3.js library.

II. Iterative Design

The biggest challenge of this project was how to organize an overwhelmingly amount of data and make some meaningful insights out of it. We wanted to allow the user to filter costs by service, state, and hospital.

Using Balsamiq mockup tooling, I designed wireframes to Identify how best to organize the various visualizations we were creating and their corresponding interactions. I later iterated on these using Adobe Illustrator. The biggest change was a step progress tool to indicate a path a user could flow to gain service cost insights while using the application. After the third iteration, I discoverd that the US map was the focal point of the site, and really did not add much value. Therefore, I removed the map and revisted the charts I chose. I ended up moving forward with a scatter plor to better uniquely identify data for each data point.


III. Development

Bradley and I translated the wireframes into the final design, which emcompasses two D3.js visualizations that can be filtered with the dropdowns in the upper portion of the websites body. Data points, when hovered upon, provide details such as hospital or cost. To keep the design consistent and quickly mock out the UI with a playful color pattern, I chose to use Materialize CSS.

Final Solution

IV. Evaluate

Overall, in this project we developed an easily accessible, web visualization tool that allows prospective US patients or caregivers to inquire average health care costs for the top most common medical services, by state or hospital. This tool provides data insights in a more accessible and transparent manner than in an excel dataset.
