uKnowHealth: Startup Weekend Hackout Project

Back
uKnowHealth is an Angular, web application aimed at simplifying health care results data for easier patient access and learning. This project is unique and special to me, because I had to learn the opposite of what I normally do as an individual contributor (developer/designer). Instead, I learned how to create and manage a business with a UX problem I had a vision to solve.

PROJECT DETAILS
Need: The general population is unable to easily access accurate health information. Searching online for health information is the most well-adopted digital health category, with 40% of those who search acting directly upon what they find.
Objective: Enable the general population to receive health care results explained in a simple, meaningful manner.
Approach: Developed web application that is simple, free, and efficient to use.
Project Duration: 3 days; Summer 2017
Group Members: Joanne Bruno (CEO), Jeremy Cantu (CTO), Lauren Welch (COO), Ana Cruz (Developer), Ivan Lopez (Lopez), Rob Quinn (Developer)
My Role(s): CEO, Project Champion, Speaker (for project pitch to investors panel)
Final Result: Angular 2 Web application functional prototype delivered. Project pitch to mock investor panel received overwhelmingly positive feedback. Traction was gained with several existing healthcare companies.

Process

In the healthcare industry, I find it increasingly complex to manage and understand my health care data. Before the vision for uKnowHealth occured to me, I had a close relative almost missed a very important abnormality on their health care lab result data (sent via patient portal), which could have been found more quickly if the data was reviewied digitally and translated to a more meaningful result. Luckily, this relative is has a professional degree in medicine, and was able to understand her health care results and notify the doctor for a follow up. Their advanced health literacy saved their life.

This situation gave me the following questions: What if this patient had not had the health literacy to be able to understand this data? How can we better enable the general population to increase their health literacy and understand their health care results?

I. Plan, Identify, Understand

At startup weekend Hackout, I created a balanced team of strong developers, and a subject matter expert in healthcare. After working through some market research, we found a few interesting points that we wanted to expand on:

(1) Searching online for health information is the most well-adopted digital health category, with 40% of those who search acting directly upon what they find.
(2) There has been a steady increase in mis diagnoses in recent years. Majority of diagnostic errors could have been prevented if an AI tool could analyze systems and help doctors arrive at a diagnoses.

We put these ideas to the test by conducting a survey in the Austin Domain area. We wanted to identify basic health literacy and where the users go to understand their numbers. Through this study, out of 26 responses, over 61% of patients attempt to search online for more information on their healthcare results. We identified that the majority of respondees were not able to identify what level of blood sugar or cholestoral is high, indicating lower health literacy than expected. When prompted if they would pay for a service that allowed them to better understand their medical information, about 50% suggested that it cost between $15-$90 per month, and others suggested it be free.

Based on our findings, our team worked together to develop wireframes for a web application that would allow the use to receive more meaningful results based on their health care data results. The user enters a query of a medical term such as "cholesterol" and enters their data from their most recent lab result, including standardization infomration such as sex, age, etc. The application then lists a visualization slider with color indication of the result based on how it falls within the data values for their selection. Based on their result, articles or futher information from partnerships will show at the bottom of the screen to further educate patient on what their results mean.

II. Create Business Case

Team member Lauren Welch and I ran through our business model to solidify our vision and marketable value. This was a challenging exercise that thoroughly helped me understand what UX problem we are trying to solve and who is it really going to help. I want to use this same methodology for any problem I solve going forward to focus in on the problem statement, use case, and have a solid deliverable.


III. Development

As the team consisted of members with varying degrees of development experience and fields of expertise, it was decided upon to utilize the Angular 2 library for creation of the high-fidelity prototype. The library's component-based workflow allowed members to quickly ideate, create, and integrate each particular slice of "functionality" for the prototype, as well as give the opportunity for the team to get familiar with a relatively cutting edge web-development toolset. This included using the Angular CLI for extremely quick boilerplate code creation, as well as the Angular Material library and design system to derive much of our end-user interface. IV. Evaluate

I delivered a 5 minute pitch to a mock investors panel. The judges asked us many questions on how we were going to get this item to market, knowing that the healthcare industry is highly complex versus other industries. The judges were impressed on how much we delivered over the course of a weekend versus the other teams.
Overall, we developed a recognized business model, a functional prototype for a web application, a successul project pitch. The biggest challenge was narrowing down the scope of the project to a single, solidified idea that the end user would find valuable. However, we overcame this through extensive market research and entrepreneurial exercises. I firmly believe that our final product deliverable will help health care results be made simple and meaningful.
